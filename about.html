About Registry Route

What is Registry Route?

Registry Route is an open B2B routing directory built for enterprise underwriting workflows and corporate entity discovery. It provides direct, verified routing to all 50 US state Secretary of State portals and corporate reinstatement pathways — organized for commercial lenders, ISOs, and underwriting teams who need fast, authoritative access to entity status data.

Registry Route is not a government agency, legal advisor, or data reseller. It is a structured directory of publicly available state resources, curated and maintained to serve commercial finance professionals conducting due diligence, entity verification, and standing checks at scale.

Verification Methodology

Data Sources

Every state portal link in Registry Route is sourced directly from the official Secretary of State website for that jurisdiction. Links are verified by manual review against the official state government domain — no third-party aggregators, no data brokers. Each state entry includes its primary business entity search portal, plus reinstatement pathway information where applicable.

Verification Process

State portal URLs are verified by loading the official state .gov or state-specific domain, confirming the landing page is an active business entity search interface, and cross-referencing against NASCIO (National Association of State Chief Information Officers) member directories. Entries flagged as Mail-In Required reflect verified state agency policy at time of last review.

Update Cadence

The directory is reviewed on a rolling quarterly basis, with priority updates triggered by state-level portal migrations, domain changes, or reinstatement law amendments. The build timestamp shown in the footer reflects the most recent production deployment. If you identify a stale or broken link, please contact support.

Who Uses Registry Route?

Registry Route is designed for commercial finance professionals who need fast, reliable access to state corporate registries as part of their underwriting and compliance workflow:

  • Alternative lenders and MCA funders verifying entity good standing before advancing capital
  • ISOs and brokers routing deal packages across multiple jurisdictions simultaneously
  • Underwriting analysts conducting pre-approval due diligence on commercial applicants
  • Compliance officers tracking reinstatement status for portfolio entities
  • Corporate attorneys and paralegals performing multi-state entity status checks
